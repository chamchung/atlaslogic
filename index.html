Why students fail

Most LSAT prep is broken by design.

The platforms that exist were built around content delivery. Atlas is built around learning.

Passive studying
Watching videos and re-reading notes feels productive. It isn't. Real learning requires active retrieval and structured reflection — not consumption.
No structure
Random drilling is the enemy of improvement. Without a coherent curriculum sequence, students repeat mistakes and never build the underlying logic skills.
No accountability
Between tutoring sessions, most students drift. Atlas tracks every missed question, every pattern, every week — and your tutor sees all of it.
